21-Day Tai Chi Program

A gentle journey from beginner to confident practitioner

Week 1: Foundation Week 2: Flow Week 3: Deepening

Week 1: Foundation

Learning to stand, breathe, and find your center

1

Finding Your Root

10 minutes

Wu Chi Standing Posture

Today we learn the foundational stance of all Tai Chi—the "empty" or "primordial" posture that connects you to the earth.

Wu Chi Stance

  1. Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, toes pointing forward
  2. Soften your knees slightly—never lock them
  3. Relax your shoulders and let arms hang naturally
  4. Tuck your chin slightly, as if holding a small peach
  5. Imagine a golden thread gently lifting your crown toward the sky
  6. Breathe naturally and hold for 2-3 minutes
Breathe
Breathing Pattern:
Inhale slowly through your nose for 4 counts.
Exhale gently through your mouth for 6 counts.
Let your belly expand naturally with each breath.
Senior Tip Stand near a wall or sturdy chair for confidence. You can also practice this seated—simply sit at the edge of your chair with feet flat on the floor.
2

Awakening the Breath

12 minutes

Belly Breathing (Dan Tian Breathing)

The Dan Tian, located about two inches below your navel, is considered your energy center. Today we learn to breathe into this vital area.

Seated Belly Breathing

  1. Sit comfortably or stand in Wu Chi posture
  2. Place both hands gently on your lower belly
  3. Inhale slowly—feel your belly expand like a balloon
  4. Exhale slowly—feel your belly gently deflate
  5. Continue for 10 breaths, keeping shoulders relaxed
  6. Rest and notice how you feel
Senior Tip If you have trouble feeling your belly move, try lying down on your back with a light book on your abdomen. Watch the book rise and fall.
3-7

Days 3-7: Building Your Foundation

12-20 min each
  1. Day 3: Loosening the Joints — Gentle warm-up for neck, shoulders, wrists, hips, knees, ankles
  2. Day 4: Sinking & Rising — Basic weight shifting from side to side
  3. Day 5: Lifting the Sky — Raising and lowering arms with breath
  4. Day 6: Embracing the Tree — Standing meditation posture
  5. Day 7: Week 1 Integration — Combining all foundation practices

Week 2: Flow

Connecting movements with breath and intention

8

Parting the Wild Horse's Mane

15 minutes

First Tai Chi Form Movement

This beautiful movement teaches coordination between upper and lower body while improving balance.

Hold the Ball

Bow Stance

  1. Start in Wu Chi, then shift weight to right leg
  2. Hold the Ball: Right hand on top, left hand below
  3. Step left foot forward at 45-degree angle
  4. Separate hands: Left arm rises, right hand lowers
  5. Shift weight forward into a gentle bow stance
  6. Alternate sides 4 times each
Senior Tip Keep your stepping foot close to the ground—no need to lift high. Take a smaller step if balance feels uncertain.
9-14

Days 9-14: Learning to Flow

12-20 min each
  1. Day 9: White Crane Spreads Wings — Open chest, improve posture
  2. Day 10: Brush Knee & Push — Walking forward with purpose
  3. Day 11: Repulse the Monkey — Graceful retreat, walking backward
  4. Day 12: Cloud Hands — Meditative side-stepping movement
  5. Day 13: Seated Cloud Hands — Chair Tai Chi variation
  6. Day 14: Week 2 Integration — Flowing practice sequence

Week 3: Deepening

Refining movements and building your personal practice

15-21

Days 15-21: Mastery & Independence

15-25 min each
  1. Day 15: Golden Rooster Stands on One Leg — Balance challenge
  2. Day 16: Fair Lady Works Shuttles — Diagonal stepping
  3. Day 17: Single Whip — The signature Tai Chi posture
  4. Day 18: Snake Creeps Down — Building leg strength
  5. Day 19: Closing Form — Gathering and storing energy
  6. Day 20: Building Your Daily Practice — Creating your routine
  7. Day 21: Complete Practice & Celebration — Full flowing session!

After 21 days, you'll have the foundation for a lifetime practice. Remember: Tai Chi is not about perfection—it's about showing up and nurturing your wellbeing.

Practice Space Setup Checklist

Prepare your perfect Tai Chi environment

Creating a dedicated practice space—even a small one—signals to your body and mind that it's time for Tai Chi. Click each item to check it off as you prepare your space.

Space Requirements

Clear floor area of at least 6×6 feet Essential

Enough room to step forward, backward, and to each side with arms extended

Remove tripping hazards Essential

Clear away rugs with curled edges, electrical cords, small furniture, and pet toys

Non-slip flooring surface Essential

Wood, tile, or low-pile carpet. Avoid slick surfaces or thick, plush rugs

Minimum Practice Space

6 feet (1.8m) 6 feet (1.8m) step left step right step back step forward

Safety & Support

Sturdy chair nearby Essential

A stable chair without wheels for balance support or seated practice

Good lighting Essential

Bright enough to see clearly, but soft and non-glaring. Natural light is ideal

Wall within arm's reach Recommended

Position yourself so you can touch a wall if needed during balance exercises

What to Wear

Loose, comfortable clothing Essential

Stretchy pants, relaxed shirts. Nothing tight around waist or chest

Flat, flexible shoes or bare feet Essential

Tai Chi shoes, soft slippers, or clean socks. Avoid heels or thick soles

Breathing Essentials

Inhale 4
Exhale 6

1 Breathe through nose, exhale through mouth

2 Expand belly on inhale, soften on exhale

3 Inhale rising/opening, exhale sinking/closing

4 Never hold your breath—keep it flowing

Golden Rules

Move Slowly

Slower is better. Feel each movement.

Stay Safe

Keep support within reach always.

Soft Knees

Never lock knees. Keep slightly bent.

Relax Shoulders

Drop shoulders away from ears.
